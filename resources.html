Market Research

Industry Benchmarks

The data sources powering the simulator's income calculations and cost-of-living baseline.

Streaming Data

Spotify Loud & Clear Report

Spotify's annual transparency report breaks down how streaming royalties are distributed across artist tiers. The data confirms that the vast majority of streaming income goes to a small percentage of artists — reinforcing why audience size and CPM tier matter so much in the simulator.

Cost of Living

MIT Living Wage Calculator

The cost-of-living baseline in the simulator is derived from MIT's Living Wage Calculator, which models the minimum income needed to cover basic expenses by city. Housing, food, transport, medical, and civic costs are all sourced from this dataset.

Industry Revenue

RIAA Music Industry Revenue Data

The Recording Industry Association of America tracks how revenue is split across streaming, live performance, and physical and digital sales. The three-stream model in Streamonomics mirrors these industry-standard categories.

Design Research

Design Decisions

The visual and UX choices that define the Streamonomics experience.

Identity

Logo & Visual Identity

The Streamonomics mark was designed around the concept of playability. The vertical bars reference both an audio equalizer and a bar chart — sitting at the intersection of music and data. The form is minimal and scalable, readable at any size without losing its connection to sound.

Interface

UI Philosophy

Competitor research revealed that existing tools for artists — royalty calculators, income estimators, tour planners — are almost universally overwhelming. Streamonomics was deliberately designed to do the opposite: one decision at a time, progressive disclosure, and a result that speaks for itself.

Color System

Color & Palette

White and gold were chosen intentionally. White signals clarity and transparency — this tool shows you the real numbers, nothing hidden. Gold references monetary incentive and creative ambition: the reason any artist is asking the question in the first place.

Simulation Outcomes

Artist Personas

Based on the results of your simulation, Streamonomics assigns you one of three artist personas. Each reflects a different approach to building a sustainable music career — shaped by how you weight streaming, live performance, and merchandise.

Persona 01

The Cataloger

Builds slow, passive income through streaming catalogs and playlists. Patient, consistent, and playing the long game — your music works for you while you sleep.

Persona 02

The Showman

Lives for the stage. Converts fans in person through high-energy live performance. The crowd is your currency — high effort, high reward.

Persona 03

The Brand Builder

Turns fan loyalty into direct revenue through merch, content, and direct-to-fan products. Your audience doesn't just listen — they invest in you.

User Research

User Interviews

Feedback from three independent users who tested the simulator prototype. Their responses shaped the current experience.

Anonymous User 01

The tool has strong potential — but inputs need grounding

"The experience feels more like filling out a model than exploring a system."

Takeaway: Inputs like audience size and fan strength felt abstract without real-world reference points. Wants to see a default outcome first and adjust from there — exploration over configuration.

Anonymous User 02

Engaging once results appear — onboarding needs acceleration

"It became more engaging when I started to see how the income changed, especially in the touring section."

Takeaway: The step-by-step flow felt slow before any results appeared. Responded well to direct cause-and-effect feedback. Wants the system to suggest what to try next, not just report what happened.

Anonymous User 03

Strong concept — persona and identity could build throughout

"The persona is only introduced at the end, which feels like a missed opportunity."

Takeaway: Wants stronger emotional feedback throughout — not just at the results screen. Sees potential in pushing toward a life-simulation feel where identity builds progressively, not all at once.
